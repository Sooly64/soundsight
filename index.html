SoundSight

Navigating the world through sound

The Challenge

⚠

The Problem

Over 2.2 billion people live with vision impairment worldwide. Everyday navigation — crossing streets, finding doors, locating objects — remains dangerous and difficult without constant assistance.

◉

Our Solution

SoundSight uses real-time computer vision to detect objects, obstacles, and spatial cues — then converts them into intuitive audio signals the user can understand instantly.

How It Works

1

Capture

A Raspberry Pi camera continuously captures live video frames of the user's surroundings.

2

Detect

YOLOv8 runs real-time object detection — identifying and labeling every object in the frame with bounding boxes.

3

Speak

Newly detected objects are instantly announced through audio — "I see chair, door, person" — so the user knows what's around them.

Built for the Radhwa IT Fair 2026

SoundSight was created originally for the Science Fair, then adapted for the IT Fair to explore how AI and sound can bridge the accessibility gap for visually impaired people.
